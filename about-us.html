About Me

✻

My name is Dillon Dragomir and I’m a Sophomore Computer Engineering student at the University of Cincinnati!! One thing I really enjoy doing with my time is binge watching Netflix with my friends and staying up till 3 AM every night studying in the engineering building. I am passionate about giving back to my community, scientific research and gaining further knowledge of a concept.

Throughout the past year I have made some amazing friends at UC and been afforded the opportunity to study my spring semester in Singapore. While in Singapore, I got to experience some life changing cultural differences that has made me a more accepting and diversified person. Also, in Singapore, I have been involved in research for a laboratory at UC called IMS, that conducts machine learning and artificial intelligence research. I have been working on a project to create a model that predicts the likelihood of injury of a girl’s soccer player from big data sets and blood work. This data is extracted from sensors and GPS locators attached to the women’s soccer players. I plan to continue conducting research that brings about new knowledge and helps make a difference in the scientific community. Finally, I enjoy reading other scientist’s findings and using their own work to help me model how I like to conduct my own research.

Into the future, I am striving to find a job my spring semester that fulfills my drive to find meaning in life. I hope to find a job in a moderately large city because I love the life, culture, music, and art a large city has to offer. Preceding my undergrad, I plan to pursue a PHD and eventually work towards running my own laboratory and working at a university. As of now, I am currently pursuing an undergraduate degree in Computer Engineering and taking any opportunities that come my way that can help me develop as a person and get me closer to attaining my personal goals.
